Kiwi

A VR Auction Platform for lovers of anmie, comics and games to sell or exchange game equipment and related products.

Summary


Kiwi is a VR Auction Platform for lovers of anmie, comics and games to sell or exchange game equipment and related products prototyped in Alibaba user experience design workshop. Based on Virtual Reality technology, Kiwi produces fun interactive experience, fulfilling the demands of the large group of Otaku.

Background


Alibaba, a well-known Chinese e-commerce company, is trying to tap the potential of markets concerning auction and to employ Virtual Reality technology for better user experience.

Our product was aimed to:

  1. Tap into potential auction areas;
  2. Stimulate user activity in auction;
  3. Enhance the Commodity category and quantity as well as the auction volume;
  4. Promote user interaction during the auction;
  5. Make the auction experience fun, engaging, and novel.

Considering the growing group of lovers of anmie, comics and games and the strong relation between games and VR, we targeted at transaction of game products and designed Kiwi.

Process

Market Discovery

  • Online games are bound to go through a revolution from traditional mouse and keyboard operation to VR control.
  • There are a quantity of online game players. Considering their own social nature, they are expected to be active on the platform.

Anime and manga fans have a high level of consumption on related products.

VR market is maturing.

Market Research

The potential market for VR gaming deals is huge in that the game commodities have advantages over traditional lots in the quantity of categories and the volume of transactions. Moreover, this market is currently not fully tapped or involved. It is worthwhile to design a brand new product for this area.

The average price of game items is one of the top in all genres.

The number of subtypes of game items is one of the top in all genres, but valid indexing method is exile.

The conversion from game forum to e-commerce platform is considerable.

User Research

Game purchase amount is considerable.

Most players prefer functional equipments to enhance the level or the ability of the game characters.

Most players are reluctant to use the auction house in games, with a contributing factor that the lots are expensive and insufficient.

There are abundant social activities in the game.

Competitive Analysis

We found some shortcomings ...

  • The lack of sociality makes it difficult to gather players.
  • The lack of auction elements undermined the sense of accomplishment and superiority for the transaction.
  • Timeliness is poor.
  • The process is boring.

We also found some merits ...

  • Seller access mechanism.
  • Bidding ladder mechanism.
  • Agent mechanism.
